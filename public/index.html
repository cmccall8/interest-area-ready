ART

SETUP
At least 1 drawing material accessible to children at least 24 months of age

EX: sufficient amount of usable crayons or markers in usable condition along with paper and drawing surface NA PERMITTED

Most art activities allow children to use materials in their own way

coloring pages not used; children not asked to copy an example

No toxic or unsafe material are accessible to children

ex: shaving cream, permanent markers, choking hazards, etc.

INTERACTIONS
Children are not required to participate

Alternate activity available

While materials are being used, do you pay close attention (appropriate to the age and ability of the children)

ex: stop vchildren from interfering with each others projects, missusing materials, making messes

Do you name colors as children use them?

Must be observed at least once

Do you breifly talk to the children about their artwork?

Ex: "What did you draw?"; "I like your red circle"

EXCELLENCE
Art materials and activities are used to teach children concepts, such as comparing color, shape, size

Must be observed at least once

Do you used words and actions to teach children to use art materials

Ex: show how to roll play dough into a ball; Must be observed at least once

Do you talk extensively with the children about their artwork?

Ex: "Tell me about your picture"; "You drew lots of circles, see how the lines go all the way around?"; Msut be observed at least twice

Do you write captions about artwork dictated by interested children 24 months and older?

Ex: "You said 'ball'! See, I wrote down what you said"; NA PERMITTED

MUSIC & MOVEMENT

SETUP
At least 10 music materials are accessible

EX: simple musical instruments, toys, rattles, music player or radio playing appropriate music

Background music does not interfere with any other activity (or is not used)

INTERACTIONS
Music and movement activities are individualized.

ex: quiet lullabies sung to sleepy child, sing hand washing song as child washes; Must be observed at least once

Staff-led group music activities are pleasant and children are generally engaged

NA Permitted

Some movement/dance activity is observed

EX: finger plays; clapping/moving to a rhythm

Children are not required to participate in group music activities if not interested

Alternative activities available; NA Permitted

EXCELLENCE
Age appropriate music intruments are available to children 2 and older

NA Permitted

Do you draw attention to features of the music?

Ex: point out rhyming works in lyrics; connect movements to fast/slow tempos; Must be observed once

Does some of the muscic/movement activities relate to current classroom themes or topics of interest?

Ex: animal songs with related movements; holiday songs

BLOCKS

SETUP
Enough blocks are accessible for each age group being observed

At least 5 accessories for blocks are accessible for children 24 months and older

NA Permitted

Blocks and accessories are organized by type

Block interest center for older toddlers/twos set aside, with storage and suitable building surface

EX: Flat rug, table, area out of traffic

INTERACTIONS
Do you show interest and talk to children about their block play?

ex: Ask about what they are building; talk about how blocks fall down; stop quarrels; must be observed at least twice

EXCELLENCE
Large hollow blocks are available for use in a suitably large area where play for toddlers/twos can be active

NA Permitted

Do you talk about age appropriate concepts related to block play in ways that interest the children

Ex: compare color, size, shape, structures; count blocks whilev stacking; discuss texture of blocks

Do you demonstrate creating simple structures while describing what you are doing?

Ex: build roads, bridges, towers, etc.; Must be observed at least once

DRAMATIC PLAY

SETUP
Many varied appropriate dramatic play materials are accessible, including dolls and soft animals

Materials represent what children experience in everyday life

EX: household routines, work, transportation

Some appropriate dress-up items are accessible for toddlers/twos

EX: hats or purses; NA Permitted

Dramatic play interest center for toddlers/twos is uses, containing materials organized by typed and some child-sized play furniture

NA Permitted

INTERACTIONS
Do you supervise dramatic play to stop major conflicts or dangerous behavior?

Do you name some of the objects children experience in their dramatic play?

Must be observed at least twice

Do you interact possitively (never negatively) while children engage in dramatice play?

EX: help with dress-up items, model how to use materials, etc.; Must be observed at least twice

EXCELLENCE
At least 4 dramatic play examples are accessible that clearly represent diveresity

EX: dolls representing different races/cultures, equiptment used by people with dissabilities, clothes and pretend food from different cultures

Materials provided for toddlers/twos to use in more active dramatic play outdoors or in another large area

NA Permitted

Do you frequently join in the children play, adding language appropriately?

Ex: name foods in childs home language and program language; assume a supporitve role in dramtic play initiated by children, enhacing but not taking over; Must be observed at least 3 separate times

MATH/NUMBER

SETUP
Many appropriate math/number materials are accessible

EX: materials that show size, shape, or number

INTERACTIONS
Math is NOT used in a punative manner

Ex: child threatened with timeout if they do not stop talking bt the time the teacher counts to 5

Do you correctly compare shapes, quantity, or size?

Ex: Use "more" or "less" when reffering to groups and "smallest" or "biggest" when reffering to objects. Must be observed at least once

Do you count objects for children in an engaging manner?

EX: playfully count childs toes, count crackers as they are put onto a childs plate, point to or move blocks as they are counted

Do you use number songs, chants, nursery rhymes, or finger plays with children in an engaging manner?

Must be observed at least once

EXCELLENCE
Do you help twos become aware of what printed numbers mean?

EX: point to a number in a counting book and count the objects; Must be observed at least once; NA Permitted

Do you use math words to describe the sequence of daily events?

EX: "first we put on our coats, second we go outdoors

Do you show number of fingers when using number talk with children?

Must be observed at least once
